Different anatomy

Operating around the brain, spine, skull base, and peripheral nerves requires different approaches, tools, and risk profiles. The anatomy alone pushes the field into specialized tracks.

Different diseases

A ruptured aneurysm, a spinal deformity, a brain tumor, and childhood hydrocephalus call for very different treatment strategies. Subspecialties help teams build deep repetition and judgment.

Different techniques

Modern neurosurgery spans microsurgery, endovascular treatment, navigation, stereotaxy, neurostimulation, endoscopy, and complex reconstruction, often under one departmental umbrella.
